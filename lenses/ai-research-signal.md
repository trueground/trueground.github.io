# AI Research Signal (local copy)

See the canonical version at `~/.claude/lenses/ai-research-signal.md`.

This copy is here so agents running in this project directory can reference it without needing the global path.

**Principle:** A finding's coverage is driven by announcement cadence, lab brand, and whether it confirms prevailing narratives — not by its actual implications. Identifying signal requires inverting the attention economy.

Ask: *Does this finding challenge an assumption the field is currently operating on, or does it confirm one?*

## Quick reference — significance signals

**High signal:**
- Negative result: standard approach shown to fail
- Convergent: same finding from 3+ independent groups
- Mechanistic: explains *why* something works, not just *that* it works
- Cross-domain: finding applies across two fields that don't read each other
- Low coverage, high disruption: few upvotes but would require practitioners to change behavior

**Low signal:**
- New SOTA on a benchmark the field has been saturating
- Confirms what everyone already believed
- From a major lab with a blog post and 500 HN upvotes
- Benchmark improvement without mechanistic explanation

## Coverage inversion rule

When evaluating whether a paper is under-covered: low upvotes + substantive claim + no lab blog post = strong candidate. The attention economy is a noise-generation machine. Do not use coverage as a quality filter.
